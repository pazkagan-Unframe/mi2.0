Principle 01
Per-lease per-its-area comparison
Each lease compares against the average of the specific sub-market it sits in — never against a blended portfolio average.
Principle 02
Sub-market default, user widens
Default is the lowest granularity available. Below 10 comps, surface widen-to-market option but never auto-apply.
Principle 03
Two-section dashboard
Portfolio-vs-market analytics and pure-market data are different use cases that should not share the same scroll.

Open decisions for the call
Decision 01
Comp threshold
10 comps as the high/medium boundary, 5 as the medium/low boundary — does Nir's data support this?
Decision 02
Cascade order on widen
Sub-market + property type → market + property type. Or include a class-relaxation step in between?
Decision 03
NER vs headline rent
Pending Khushboo's lease abstraction test. Show both with a metric toggle, or pick one?
Decision 04
Per-row vs table-level scope
Each lease independently resolves scope, or one toggle applies to the whole table?
Decision 05
Action layer connection
Renegotiation pool KPI → does clicking it create a transaction in the Cockpit, or just filter the table?
Decision 06
Module name
"Intelligence" with two tabs, or split into "Portfolio Intelligence" and "Market Intelligence" as separate nav items?
